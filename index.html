Studio Philosophy

Designed around what the land is already trying to become.

Gridless Wilds works with wild-edge residential properties where standard landscape design feels too polished, too suburban, or too disconnected from the site. We create outdoor spaces that hold structure without erasing the character of the land.

01

Restraint over decoration

We use fewer, stronger moves: paths, stone, planting, shade, grading, and outdoor rooms that feel inevitable.

02

Native where it matters

Planting is selected for climate, habitat, seasonal movement, and long-term resilience.

03

Built to be managed clearly

Every project moves through a documented design-build process powered by FieldDesk.

Trades

Homes, landscapes, construction, and fabrication for rural luxury properties.

Gridless Wilds Designs creates conceptual modern home, landscape, construction, and custom metalwork systems for properties where architecture meets wild land. Designed as one system, powered by FieldDesk.

01

Modern Home Concepts

Modern rural homes designed for the site first — form, orientation, materials, and detailing that answer to terrain, weather, and view before style. This is where every project begins.

Includes
  • Site and massing studies
  • Modern rural home concepts
  • Material and facade palettes
  • Blackened steel and timber detailing
  • Glass-at-dusk and daylight strategy
  • Indoor–outdoor transitions
  • Guest houses, pavilions, and gear barns
  • Coordination with architects of record
02

Landscape Design + Construction

Full-property landscape design and the construction to build it — from concept plans and native planting to terraces, grading, stone, and outdoor rooms.

Includes
  • Site analysis and concept plans
  • Native and climate-adapted planting
  • Outdoor living, fire terraces, and courts
  • Stone terraces, steps, and seat walls
  • Grading, drainage, and slope stabilization
  • Arrival sequences and gravel drives
  • Meadow restoration and shoreline planting
  • Construction sequencing and coordination
03

Custom Metal Fabrication

Detailed in steel. Shop-drawn and fabricated metalwork that ties architecture, landscape, and construction together — corten, blackened steel, and weathered barn metal. Equal to every other trade, never an add-on.

Includes
  • Shop drawings and fabrication detailing
  • Custom gates, screens, and monuments
  • Railings, stairs, and structural steel
  • Fire features and shade frames
  • Corten and blackened steel elements
  • Oversized doors and custom hardware
  • Finish and weathering strategy
  • Field-fit coordination through FieldDesk
04

Studio Philosophy

Keep the land wild, but make it livable.

A few beliefs that shape every decision we make on a property.

01

Structure without sterilizing the site

The strongest landscapes hold just enough structure to be lived in — paths, stone, grading, outdoor rooms — without scrubbing the wildness out of the land. We look for the fewest, most durable moves that make a property usable.

02

Outdoor rooms that feel discovered, not dropped in

A good terrace or fire room should feel like it was always going to be there — tucked into grade, framed by planting, sited to the view. We design outdoor rooms to feel found rather than installed.

03

Wild-friendly does not mean unmanaged

There is a difference between a landscape left alone and one designed to feel that way. Native planting, meadow edges, and habitat still deserve legibility, intention, and long-term care.

04

The client experience should feel as composed as the finished landscape

Design is only half of it. From first inquiry to final walkthrough, the process should feel calm and clear — which is exactly why every project runs on FieldDesk.

FAQs

Questions before we walk the site.

The things clients most often want to understand before starting a project.

What types of properties does Gridless Wilds work on?

We focus on high-end residential properties where the built environment meets wild land — mountain retreats, desert estates, lakefront homes, ranch compounds, woodland homes, and rural residences. If a property has terrain, views, drainage, wildlife, or native land worth designing around, it is the kind of site we do our best work on.

Do you only work on rural properties?

No. While much of our work is rural and wild-edge, we also take on refined suburban conversions — native front yards, pollinator plantings, and low-maintenance landscapes that read as intentional rather than simply un-mowed. The common thread is restraint and a respect for the site, not the zip code.

Do you provide installation?

We are a design-build-coordination studio. We produce the design and construction documentation and coordinate the build through our process and crews, with every phase tracked in FieldDesk. On projects where you already have a trusted builder, we coordinate directly with them.

Can you coordinate with my architect or builder?

Yes, and we prefer to be involved early. We regularly work alongside architects, builders, and specialty contractors, and FieldDesk gives every party one shared place for schedules, approvals, field documentation, and decisions so nothing falls through the gaps between trades.

Do you use native plants?

Native and climate-adapted planting is central to how we work. We select plants for climate, habitat, seasonal movement, and long-term resilience — not just for a single season of color. Native where it matters is one of our core principles.

Can you design for low-water landscapes?

Absolutely. Water-wise and xeric planting, meadow restoration, dry washes, and drainage-sensitive grading are all core parts of our desert, mountain, and rural work. We design landscapes to be durable and low-maintenance over the long term.

What is FieldDesk?

FieldDesk is the project management and client portal system used to organize Gridless Wilds projects. It gives clients and teams one place to view updates, schedules, approvals, field photos, budgets, and project documentation — so the messy middle of a design-build project stays visible and calm.

Will I get access to a client portal?

Yes. Every managed project includes a FieldDesk client portal where you can follow updates, review approvals, see photos, check the schedule, and read a clear budget summary — without digging through email chains. You can explore a live demo of that portal from our FieldDesk pages.

Can I see project photos and updates during construction?

Yes. Site photos, field notes, weather conditions, and progress updates are attached to your project record in FieldDesk and surfaced in your client portal. You will always know where the project stands and what is coming next.

How are budgets handled?

Budgets live in FieldDesk alongside the rest of the project. We track allowances, selections, change orders, percent complete, and overall budget health so you get a clear picture rather than a surprise. Billing and proposals are handled through billing@gridlesswilds.us.

Do you work outside your home region?

Our core service regions are Nevada, Wyoming, Montana, Idaho, Utah, Arizona, the Texas Hill Country, and the Missouri Ozarks. We also take on select remote properties by consultation when the project is the right fit — reach out and tell us about the land.

How do I start a project?

Send us the property details through our contact form, or email studio@gridlesswilds.us. We will review the site, set up a discovery call, and walk you through how the design and FieldDesk-managed build process works from there.
